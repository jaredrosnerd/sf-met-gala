The Concept

Not a party.
A happening.

The SF Met Gala is a private, friends-organized party built around the one thing SF has never really had: a proper excuse to get dressed up. No charity auction, no corporate sponsor. Just a really good night in a beautiful building with people who take fashion seriously.

The theme is Costume Art — same as this year's real Met Gala. Sculptural silhouettes, draped fabric, architectural shapes. Outfits that belong in a gallery. We're not throwing this in a museum, so we're turning the venue into one.

"This is a night for people who want to dress like the outfit belongs in a gallery."
02
The Theme

Costume Art

Dress as if your outfit were acquired by a museum. Not a Halloween costume — a wearable sculpture. Think silhouettes that question the body, materials that challenge function, and colors that have no name in any catalog.

  • Avant-garde runway looks translated into lived-in garments
  • Sculptural armor — plates, volumes, and architectural form
  • Textile as medium: paper, wire, found materials, couture technique
  • Surrealist silhouettes that make people stop and stare
  • A garment with a point of view, not just a color story

The Venue

The Conservatory
at One Sansome

Built in 1907, One Sansome is one of San Francisco's most storied Financial District buildings. The Conservatory — its crown jewel — is a soaring glass atrium flanked by 38-foot Doric columns and 8,100 square feet of primary event space. It has witnessed over a century of the city's history from behind its Beaux-Arts facade.

The light in this room is unlike anything else in San Francisco. In the evening, it turns gold. The columns cast long shadows. The glass above holds the last of the sky. It is a space designed for occasions that deserve to be remembered.
